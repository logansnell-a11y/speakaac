Speak gives nonverbal children
a voice — and a way out.

Speak is a free web-based AAC (augmentative and alternative communication) app for nonverbal and minimally verbal children and adults. It's the only AAC system in the world with a private, silent safety channel built in from day one — so a child can alert a trusted adult outside the home if they feel unsafe, without the person in the room ever knowing.

The story

Built by one person who couldn't ignore the gap

Logan Snell is 18 years old and lives in Axtell, Kansas — a town of about 400 people. He built Speak alone, over the course of several months, after researching AAC tools and encountering a consistent finding: children with communication disabilities are abused at 3 to 4 times the rate of their speaking peers, and the thing that makes them most vulnerable is the same thing keeping them from reporting it.

No AAC app has ever addressed that. Every existing product gives every child the same symbol grid, at the same price, with no consideration for what happens when the adult in the room is the problem. Speak was built specifically to close that gap.

The private safety channel — a way for a nonverbal child to silently alert a trusted adult outside the primary caretaker relationship — is built into the free version. It always will be. Safety should never require a subscription.

"The thing that makes them most vulnerable is the same thing keeping them silent. No one had built anything to change that."

— Logan Snell, founder, Speak AAC
Key talking points

What makes Speak different

The only AAC app with a built-in safety channel. When a child presses the Help button, a silent email goes immediately to a trusted contact outside the home — a grandparent, teacher, or therapist. No sound. Nothing visible on screen. The person in the room sees nothing.
Personalized by diagnosis in under 2 minutes. Answer 10 questions about the child's diagnosis, age, motor ability, and sensory needs. Speak configures the entire layout, vocabulary, symbol size, and starting categories automatically. Setup that used to take hours with an SLP takes minutes.
Free to start — no App Store required. Speak runs in any browser. Install it on an iPad or Android tablet by adding it to the home screen. No app store. No download fees. No credit card.
Built on the international AAC standard. Every symbol uses ARASAAC — the pictogram set used by SLPs and schools worldwide — so children who have used AAC before recognize everything immediately.
AI Sentence Builder. Tap a few symbols and Speak uses Claude AI (by Anthropic) to construct a full, natural sentence spoken aloud. "Want water more" becomes "I would really like some more water, please."
Research-backed problem, novel solution. The 3–4× abuse rate for children with communication disabilities is documented in peer-reviewed literature (Sullivan & Knutson, 2000). No empirical study has yet evaluated a safety-integrated AAC system — Speak is seeking a university research partner for that study.
