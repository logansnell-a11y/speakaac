| **Google.org Impact Challenge** | varies | Disability + accessibility focus. Requires LLC or fiscal sponsor (a university partner can serve as fiscal sponsor before LLC is formed). |

**After LLC:**
1. File LLC at sos.ks.gov — $165
2. Get EIN at irs.gov — free, takes 10 minutes
3. Register at SAM.gov — free, required for federal grants (SBIR, NIDILRR)

## Tier 3 — Need LLC + research partner + IRB

| Grant | Amount | Path |
|---|---|---|
| **NIDILRR Field-Initiated Projects** | $100k–$500k over 3–5 years | Dr. Brady email (nbrady@ku.edu) → partnership agreement → LLC → EIN → SAM.gov → IRB through KU → application. Realistic timeline: 12–18 months to first funding. research_concept.md is ready. |

**Why this is worth pursuing even though it's slow:**
NIDILRR is one of the few federal programs where an 18-year-old solo developer can legitimately compete — because the university partner handles the IRB, clinical access, and institutional credibility. Logan contributes the working technology. This is the "untouched funds" category — most eligible small companies don't apply because they don't know it exists for software/app companies.
