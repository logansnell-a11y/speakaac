The Speak Journey

One 18-year-old developer. One app that gives nonverbal children a voice — and a way to say I'm not safe. This is every milestone, in order.

The Beginning
Origin
Early 2026
The idea
Logan Snell, 18, Axtell Kansas, realizes that every AAC app gives every child the same generic grid — regardless of diagnosis, age, or ability. And not one of them has a private way for a child to say I'm not safe without the person in the room knowing. Speak begins.
Build
March 15, 2026
First working build
Symbol grid, gTTS audio, ARASAAC pictograms, and a working safety channel. First deploy to Netlify. The app functions.
Launch Day — March 17, 2026
Live
March 17, 2026
Speak v1.0 launches at speakaac.org
After a full build sprint: AI sentence builder powered by Claude Haiku, Supabase auth and cloud sync, Stripe payments, 17 onboarding diagnosis profiles, teacher dashboard, session history, kiosk mode, offline support, and a COPPA-compliant privacy policy. All deployed by one person in one day.

GoFundMe goes live
Community fundraising campaign launched to fund development, server costs, and the school access program — giving Speak to families and classrooms that can't afford it.

March 17, 2026
